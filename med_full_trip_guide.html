Fly open-jaw, not round-trip
  • Book: Lincoln → Valencia in, Rome → Lincoln out
  • Avoids backtracking + often same price as round-trip
  • Search Google Flights with "Add destination" for open-jaw
  • Save: no extra train back to Valencia (~$0–$50)
Amtrak to Chicago instead of flying
  • California Zephyr: Lincoln → Chicago ~$50–85 vs ~$200+ flying
  • Saves ~$230–$330 on domestic legs combined
  • No tight connection stress before long-haul flight
  • Scenic and relaxing — a good way to start the trip
Go in shoulder season
  • April–June or September–October
  • Flights: $300–$500 cheaper than July–August
  • Hostel dorms: up to 40% cheaper than peak
  • Barcelona weekday hostel ~$28 vs ~$80 on summer weekends
Skip overnight stays in Monaco + Cannes
  • Day-trip both from Nice — saves 2 nights of accommodation
  • Hostel in Monaco/Cannes = $80–$120/night
  • Nice → Monaco TER train: ~$5, no reservation needed
  • Total saved: ~$160–$240
Spain: menu del día every day
  • Available at most restaurants for lunch in Valencia + Barcelona
  • 3 courses + drink for $10–$13 vs $25–$35 for dinner
  • Make lunch your big meal — eat cheaply at breakfast + dinner
  • Estimated saving over 5 days in Spain: $50–$75
Use supermarkets everywhere
  • Mercadona (Spain), Lidl/Aldi (France + Italy) everywhere
  • Breakfast + picnic lunch from supermarket: $4–$7 total
  • Hostel kitchens: cook dinner 3–4 times across the trip
  • Estimated total food saving: $80–$120 over 17 days
TER regional trains on the Riviera
  • Marseille → Cannes → Nice: TER, no reservation, covered by Eurail
  • TGV on same stretch costs $10–$30 extra in reservation fees
  • Only takes ~30 min longer and is far more scenic
  • Save ~$30–$50 on Riviera train reservations
Free walking tours in every city
  • Available in Valencia, Barcelona, Marseille, Nice, Rome, Genoa
  • Tip-based — $5–10 is totally fine
  • Best way to get your bearings on day 1 in each city
  • Skip paid city tours ($25–$45) that cover the same ground
